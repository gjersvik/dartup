The Mission

Enable easy and frictionless hosting of Dart applications and services. In an open and community driven way.

Easy and frictionless

It should be easy for anyone to run a full stack Dart application. So your goal is to give easy choices that matters first. Still give you the power and control you need when you application grows. In a perfect world you should be able just give us code and a budget and we handle the rest.

Open Source Startup

I have done the secret build up a big project stealth mode startup and it was a pain. This time I want swing the pendulum the other way and be as open and transparent as possible. The rule of thumb should be, the only thing secret are the keys and customer data.

For the community

In the end its all about you guys and girls in the Dart community. The goal of this enterprise is to help the Dart community not to make a few of us rich. So we will seek input on almost everything. But we will not necessarily blindly follow the majority. And if the community allows it we would like to give hosting to even the ones with a zero budget to help Dart grow.

Accelerated on Amazon Web Services

Why build everything from scratch when you stand on shoulders of giants and in host/cloud business there is none bigger. With AWS as the base we can get to the market quicker and better than doing all inhouse. It also has the positive effect for you to have access to the extensive ecosystem that has buildup around AWS. This is where we will start but where we go be will mostly up to you the community.

The Roadmap

Minimum product to positive cash flow to community guided reinvestment.

Minimum product

The plan is to first create a simple hostings service for static pages created with Dart. Like webpages or games. That is something that I should be able to build quickly. It will be free of charge for a limited customer base. I dont have the money to host a flash mob out of pocket. I know that most of you want Dart server hosting. But releasing something useful early has two purposes. First to be a focus point for feedback and improvements. The second is to show myself and the world that Iâ€™m serious. Talk is cheap.

Positive cash flow.

After the initial product offering the next goal is to get to a state where I no longer pay out of pocket. To reach that will be a combination of necessary steps and implementing requested features. The trick is to complete this without increasing my cost substantially but still make something worth paying for. I have some ideas but do not want to get ahead of myself.

Community guided reinvestment.

Now we are really in business and can start to chip away on the most requested features as money and time becomes available. To create a good host and/or open source project.

Profit?

Not really a goal. The dream is to be able to work on this full time and earn a market salary. But as I see it the money that customers put in are there to make Dartup better. But you know money corrupts so we will see. Muahahaha.
